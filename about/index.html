Independent Perspectives, Grounded in Experience

Cybersecurity is often discussed through technology, products, and the latest threats. While these are important, I believe the real challenge lies elsewhere — in how organisations make decisions, govern technology, build resilience, and ultimately earn digital trust.

My name is Jaiz Anuar, and this website is my personal platform for sharing independent perspectives on cybersecurity, technology governance, digital trust, enterprise architecture, and leadership.

The articles and papers published here are based on professional experience, continuous learning, research, and personal reflection. They are intended to encourage critical thinking rather than simply provide answers.

Why This Website Exists

This website was created with a simple purpose: to share practical insights that bridge technology, governance, and business.

Many cybersecurity discussions focus on implementing the latest tools or responding to the newest threats. Here, I aim to explore the broader questions: how organisations build digital trust, what makes cybersecurity governance effective, where technology and business strategy intersect, and how leaders should make balanced, risk-informed decisions.

What You Will Find Here

This website brings together articles, research papers, frameworks, and reflections on cybersecurity, digital trust, governance, leadership, architecture, cloud security, identity, resilience, and lessons learned from the field.

Some articles may challenge conventional thinking. Others may present structured frameworks, research ideas, or practical observations. Together, they reflect an ongoing commitment to thoughtful discussion and continuous learning.

A Personal Philosophy

Technology continues to evolve, but the principles that underpin trust remain consistent. Effective cybersecurity is built on independent thinking, practical judgement, risk-informed decisions, continuous learning, transparency, and accountability.

The views expressed on this website are my own and are shared in a personal capacity. They do not represent the official views, policies, or positions of any organisation with which I am or have been associated.

Question assumptions. Share knowledge. Build trust.
